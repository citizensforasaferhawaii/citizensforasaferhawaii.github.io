MISSION

Through the principles of compassion, unity, and strength, we dare to challenge the status quo of ever growing apathy and indifference for our fellow man. Through compassion, we seek to build bridges to all members of our society; transcending societal class, race, religion, or gender. Through unity, we seek to build a strong member foundation to believe in the ideals of equality and prosperity for all. In strength, we seek to overcome the injustices of the world, daring to shine light on even the darkest corners of our society.

In action, we aim to tear down the invisible walls that divide our society. By reconnecting communities, introducing ourselves to the strangers next door; we create accountability, respect, and friendship.

For our society’s most vulnerable, we choose to believe in them, when no-one else will, even when they choose to not believe in themselves. It is through this faith that we can turn that desperate someone, down on their luck, into someone we can call: a friend, a neighbor, a teacher, etc.

Ultimately, it is through our guiding principles, our philosophy, that we choose to make a positive difference in this world. We see potential, when no one else can. We see friends, where others see enemies. We see leaders, where others see followers. We are the solution, we are Citizens for a Safer Hawaii.

WHO WE ARE

Citizens for a Safer Hawaii (CSH) was created by a group of concerned citizens whom were alarmed about the growing number of homeless, and mentally ill homeless that have been neglected by the state of Hawaii, and instead left for local residents, and tourists to deal with. Fed up by the states lack of effort, and lack of cooperation with the City and County of Honolulu, CSH was born.

CSH is an organization that focuses on creating innovative, clever, and well-thought-out solutions to the complex issues of homelessness, crime, and poverty. Fueled by our own ambitions, and guided by our principles, of compassion, unity, and strength, we dream of the day where no one ever again has to worry of going hungry, losing their home, or losing precious belongings. We dare to dream of a day where we meet strangers with kindness, and openness, instead of fear and aggression. A day where we choose to be kind to one another, choosing to act, or to help in time of anyone’s need, instead of choosing to look the other away.
